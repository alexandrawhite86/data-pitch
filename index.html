Why are food prices in New York City higher than the regional average?

Food prices in Brooklyn are significantly higher than the February 2021 Consumer Price Index Northeast Regional Average.

By Alexandra White

March 15 2021

Lead: Food Prices in Brooklyn, NY for cheddar cheese, chicken breast, white bread, bacon, ground beef, milk, and potatoes were higher than the Average Consumer Price Index February 2021 Northeast price for each product.

Nutgraph: Demand for organic food has risen in New York City, and many of the stores with the highest average prices sold organic or vegan products.

Key Points:

  1. Food Prices are higher on average than northeast regional prices, but over 1 million New York City residents are food insecure, according to Mayor de Blasio's office.
  2. The biggest reason for the high price of food in certain stores were organic products, specifically milk, cheese, and chicken.
  3. Big grocery stores like Key Food have more buying power and can afford to price food at lower prices.

Kicker: I plan to wrap up my piece by showcasing how even though the demand for organic food has driven up prices, many New York City leaders and food advocacy groups want more organic and natural foods in New York City.
